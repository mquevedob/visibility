# Project objective

## Main goal

The goal of this project is to build a product visibility intelligence platform for ecommerce merchants.

The platform should detect when products that exist in a merchant catalog are not being found by buyers in external discovery channels, explain the probable reasons, and translate that invisibility into actionable commercial insight.

In simpler terms:

> Help merchants discover lost sales caused by products that exist, but do not appear — or appear incorrectly — when customers search for them outside the merchant site.

## Core problem

Merchants often assume that if a product exists in their ecommerce catalog, buyers can find it.

That assumption is frequently false.

A product may be technically available, but commercially invisible because it is missing, suppressed, misrepresented, or poorly matched in channels such as:

- Google
- Bing
- Google Shopping
- marketplaces
- comparison engines
- AI search assistants
- future shopping/discovery agents

## What the system should answer

The final system should help answer:

- Should this product appear for this search intent?
- Does it actually appear?
- Where does it fail to appear?
- Why is it probably missing or weakly represented?
- What evidence supports that diagnosis?
- What should the merchant fix first?
- What commercial opportunity may be at risk?

## Strategic positioning

This project is not a generic SEO tool.

It is focused on product-level commercial visibility.

Traditional SEO tools usually explain rankings, backlinks, crawl issues, or page-level optimization. This project should focus on a different question:

> Are products that should be discoverable by buyers actually visible in the external places where buyers search?

## Long-term capabilities

The long-term product should combine four capabilities:

1. **Expected visibility**
   - Determine which searches, intents, and buyer questions should plausibly map to each product.

2. **Actual visibility**
   - Measure whether the product appears in external search and discovery results.

3. **Visibility diagnostics**
   - Explain probable causes such as noindex, robots blocking, canonical problems, weak metadata, missing Product schema, content mismatch, feed mismatch, JavaScript rendering issues, or wrong URLs ranking instead.

4. **Commercial impact**
   - Translate visibility failures into prioritized business insight: which products are invisible, which searches are affected, what evidence exists, and what fixes are likely to matter.

## Final vision

The final vision is to help ecommerce teams detect and reduce revenue loss caused by product invisibility.

The product should not merely say:

> Your SEO has issues.

It should say:

> These products are invisible or weakly represented for searches that buyers are likely to make; here is the evidence, the probable cause, and the recommended next action.
